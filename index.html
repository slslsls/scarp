Welcome

I'm a full-stack web developer in Seattle, WA. I started coding in late 2015 and was immediately hooked. I really enjoy the combination of creativity and logic needed to solve problems and build things in the world of software and web development. In February 2016 I began a six-month coding bootcamp, focusing on full-stack development with mostly JavaScript-based technologies like Node.js and Angular. It was extremely challenging, a lot of fun, and served as a confirmation that this is what I want to spend my time doing. Near the end of the program I was accepted into Microsoft's LEAP internship program, which exposed me to the .NET Framework, Visual Studio, and backend data-processing on a large scale. Since then I've been taking online courses to improve my skills, and working on personal projects for fun.

Outside of programming I'm interested in music composition (see SoundCloud), drawing (see Tumblr), linguistics (see my project In Other Words), economics, and astronomy. My lightning talks at Galvanize covered Planet 9, memetics, and the Berenstein Bears problem. I recently started volunteering for a non-profit called Kilowatts for Humanity, which works with developing communities to plan and implement off-grid electrical solutions. I'll be working with their dev team to plan and write software for Raspberry Pi data loggers in the field, and to maintain and improve their website.
